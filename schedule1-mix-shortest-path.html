Mixing Pathfinder

Pick a starting product and stack up as many target effects as you want. This searches the actual space of possible mixes — states are full effect sets, transitions are ingredient additions — for the shortest ingredient sequence that lands all of them on one product.

Products cap out at 8 effects — pick up to 8 targets.
Graph built from a vector-math reverse-engineering of the game's mixing logic (114 verified single-effect transformations), extended here into a full multi-effect state search.
Modeling assumption: adding an ingredient simultaneously transforms every currently-present effect that has a matching rule for it, then adds its own base effect if a slot remains (max 8 effects/product; once full, further ingredients have no effect). If real in-game behavior differs on simultaneous transforms, treat multi-target results as an upper bound and verify in-game.
